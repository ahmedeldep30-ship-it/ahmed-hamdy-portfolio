# Ahmed Hamdy Portfolio — Design & Voice System

**Status:** Current and binding from 2026-07-16  
**Works with:** `04-PORTFOLIO-DIRECTION-V4.md`  
**Purpose:** Make the portfolio feel authored, connected, contemporary, and institutionally credible without turning breadth into technical overclaiming.

---

## 1. Formula4You is evidence, not the identity

Formula4You proves founder ownership, market judgment, platform operations, customer journeys, and the ability to turn an idea into live work. It does not lead the site identity and it does not appear as the answer to every capability.

Rules:

- The home introduction does not mention Formula4You.
- The primary calls to action are selected work and role/business conversations, not the platform.
- The employer path shows Alpha Capital before Formula4You.
- The business path may use Formula4You as one relevant example, not as the whole proposition.
- Formula4You appears after Ahmed's operating point of view and core capabilities are already clear.
- The case title uses **Founder — Business Growth, Customer Experience & Platform Operations**.
- The case focuses on the market problem, participant needs, operating choices, contribution scope, and live result. Technical/product detail is supporting evidence.

The visitor should be able to understand Ahmed's value before learning that he founded Formula4You.

## 2. Authorship standard

The public writing must sound like Ahmed explaining real work to an intelligent colleague. It must not sound like a generated executive summary.

### Write like this

- Begin with a specific problem, observed behaviour, or decision.
- Use first person when the judgment or contribution is Ahmed's.
- Vary sentence length naturally.
- Prefer one useful example over a list of abstract capabilities.
- Name what changed, why it mattered, and where Ahmed's responsibility stopped.
- Use ordinary business English before specialist terminology.
- Allow a clear opinion when it is supported by reasoning.
- Keep small human details that show how the conclusion was reached.

### Avoid

- Repeated formulas such as “not X, but Y.”
- Three or more abstract nouns stacked into one sentence.
- A list of departments followed by a grand conclusion.
- Empty claims such as “driving transformation”, “unlocking growth”, or “delivering excellence”.
- Every paragraph having the same length and rhythm.
- Over-explaining honesty or repeatedly defending what is not claimed.
- Capability lists that could belong to any consultant.
- Perfectly symmetrical copy that reads like a template.

### Human-voice review

Before publication, every major section must pass these questions:

1. Could Ahmed say this naturally in a conversation?
2. Is there a real observation, example, or decision behind it?
3. Does the paragraph contain anything only Ahmed could plausibly say?
4. Is any sentence trying to sound senior instead of being useful?
5. Can a shorter, plainer sentence carry the same meaning?

## 3. Visual direction — Editorial Signal System

The site should feel like a serious operating document that has been turned into a modern digital experience. It is neither a dashboard nor a minimal résumé page.

### Visual idea

A single signal travels through the experience:

> **Customer signal → recurring pattern → root cause → operating decision → measurable change**

This appears through section numbering, a connecting rule, small status nodes, directional movement, and recurring annotation language. It should feel consistent, not decorative.

### Palette

- **Night:** `#0B1119` — primary dark background
- **Night raised:** `#141D29` — selective raised surface
- **Paper:** `#F3F0E8` — primary light background
- **Paper bright:** `#FFFDF8` — evidence and reading surface
- **Ink:** `#111927` — text on light surfaces
- **Chalk:** `#F7F5EF` — text on dark surfaces
- **Slate:** `#9EABBA` — secondary text on dark
- **Signal:** `#C5F05A` — primary action and active-system marker
- **Cobalt:** `#7185FF` — links, supporting information, and selected states
- **Friction:** `#FF826C` — risk, break, or exception only

Signal, cobalt, and friction have different meanings. They must not be used as random decoration.

### Typography

- Manrope carries names, navigation, headings, and controls.
- Inter carries operational explanation and longer reading.
- Source Serif 4 appears only for short reflective lines or a single quoted judgment.
- Headlines use fewer words at larger scale.
- Body copy is never reduced to create visual density.

### Layout

- One strong opening composition.
- Asymmetric editorial grids rather than repeating card grids.
- Full-width narrative transitions between dark and paper surfaces.
- A visible reading path with section numbers and one connecting signal line.
- Fewer containers; spacing and rules establish hierarchy.
- Evidence gets real space. Decorative UI does not compete with it.
- Formula4You screenshots remain inside the case study and do not become the site's visual identity.

### Components

- Buttons use a compact rectangular geometry, not oversized SaaS pills.
- Audience selection behaves like a clear route choice, not a product toggle.
- Capability groups are editorial rows with examples and outputs, not ten tabs.
- Case previews show context, contribution, and what the work demonstrates.
- Evidence labels are quiet annotations, not badge collections.
- Contact actions are divided clearly between roles and business enquiries.

### Motion

- Motion explains sequence or hierarchy.
- Page arrival is calm: headline, signal line, then supporting content.
- Section reveals travel no more than 12–16px and settle quickly.
- Hover motion affects an arrow, underline, or signal node; containers do not float.
- The diagnostic visual may animate once and always has a readable final state.
- Reduced-motion users receive the complete meaning without animation.

### Do not use

- glassmorphism;
- glowing cards;
- decorative dashboards;
- parallax scenes;
- animated gradients competing with content;
- repeated pills and badges;
- model-authored illustrations or ornamental SVG diagrams;
- motion that exists only to demonstrate that the page is animated.

## 4. Home-page journey

The home page should feel like one argument:

1. **Identity** — who Ahmed is and the kind of loss he finds.
2. **A worked diagnosis** — show how a small customer issue becomes an operating problem.
3. **Core depth** — four connected capabilities, each with a concrete output.
4. **Selected evidence** — Alpha Capital first, Formula4You second, no unfinished work.
5. **Working method** — how evidence becomes a decision and then a coordinated change.
6. **Audience routes** — what an employer or business should do next.
7. **Career narrative** — why Ahmed sees problems this way.
8. **Contact** — one clear next action for the visitor's context.

The home page is not a directory of every future article, playbook, or service.

---

## 5. Quality bar

The finished experience must pass five tests:

1. A recruiter understands Ahmed's role and depth in under 30 seconds.
2. A business owner recognizes at least one problem Ahmed can help diagnose.
3. No page implies visual-design, engineering, advanced-analytics, or AI-architecture ownership.
4. The site remains coherent if Formula4You is removed from the first two screens.
5. Every visual decision improves reading, orientation, proof, or action.
