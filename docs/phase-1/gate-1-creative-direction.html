Structural finding — designed for, not discovered in QA

Navy (L ≈ 0.021713) and charcoal (L ≈ 0.021742) are luminance-identical. Heading-versus-body hierarchy therefore cannot rely on contrast — it comes from size, weight, and spacing. Their hue separation only reads at large sizes. Every heading in this system earns its hierarchy typographically.

07

Simplify → Automate → Build

The AI-aware decision framework. Its point is knowing when not to automate or build.

Gate 1 · always first
Simplify

Remove the work. Fix the sentence, the field, the owner, the policy. Most problems end here — and the ones that do were never technology problems.

  • Is the cause content, policy, ownership, or process?
  • Can a clearer sentence remove the contact entirely?
  • What stops existing if we do nothing?
Gate 2 · only if it recurs
Automate

Only after the work is already simple. Automating a bad process makes the waste faster and harder to see.

  • Is it frequent, repeatable, and stable?
  • Is the data ready and trustworthy?
  • Is human judgment genuinely absent?
  • What is the cost of being confidently wrong?
Gate 3 · last resort
Build

Only when simplifying and automating both fail, and the value clears the total cost — including who maintains it in year two.

  • Implementation cost vs. ongoing operating cost
  • Who owns it after launch?
  • How will success be measured — and when do we stop?
  • What does it make harder to change later?

The framework's real argument

AI awareness is not enthusiasm. The support playbook's strongest moment is leadership considering a chatbot, and the disciplined answer being rewrite the article title. The decision not to automate is the proof of judgment — and it's the one thing a generated portfolio never says.

Every gate evaluates

Recurrence and frequency · business impact · root cause · information and data readiness · whether human judgment is required · risk of incorrect automation · implementation cost · ongoing operating and maintenance cost · ownership after launch · success measurement.
