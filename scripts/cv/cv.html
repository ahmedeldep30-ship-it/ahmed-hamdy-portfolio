Promoted after six months on the frontline to manage a 13-person function comprising 10 agents, 2 senior agents and 1 QA specialist, running day-to-day support on Intercom, Discord and email.

  • Rebuilt the ticket taxonomy to separate the stated reason for contact from the cause found after investigation, plus the affected feature, journey stage, owning department and whether the contact was preventable — replacing four generic buckets that had collapsed product, bank, provider and instruction failures into one reporting bar.
  • Authored the customer-facing knowledge base and FAQ articles, prioritised by repeat-contact rate, handle time, escalation rate and complaint severity.
  • Specified an in-product withdrawal status timeline after establishing that the highest-volume contact driver was a visibility failure rather than a delay. Repeat contacts on that driver dropped clearly.
  • Replaced script-adherence QA with decision-quality review: whether the agent reached correct information, explained it usably, named the next step and recorded the cause. Separated ticket resolution from problem resolution as distinct measures.
  • Redesigned queue routing and priority on financial impact, customers affected, time sensitivity and compliance risk rather than on contact channel. Defined escalation paths and response standards so ownership of an exception was explicit and answers stayed consistent.
  • Identified and drove the fix for a payment status-mapping defect in which the provider's "accepted for processing" acknowledgement was recorded as final settlement, so customers were told funds had arrived while still in flight. Assembled the incident evidence, coordinated Payments Operations, Engineering and Finance, and introduced an intermediate status, a monitoring alert and an incident playbook.
  • Worked across sales, operations and product on the upstream causes of recurring contact rather than on reply speed.
